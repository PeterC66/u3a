Theatre Trips 2 Operation Mincemeat, Milton Keynes Wednesday 10 th June There are still seats available for this show, which started out as a fringe musical about an outlandish war plan and became a West End and Broadway smash. While Operation Mincemeat is undoubtedly brilliant, hilarious, moving and astonishing, sometimes all in the space of one dance routine, the story its based on doesnt exactly scream: Hit stage musical!. The actual Operation Mincemeat was a crack piece of second world war deception. MI5 took the body of homeless Welsh labourer Glyndwr Michael, who had died after eating rat poison, gave him a new name (Major William Martin), armed him with fake plans about an allied invasion of Sardinia and Greece (Sicily was the real target), and deposited his uniformed body on a beach in Huelva, Spain, hoping it would reach German spies. Split Lip, the four men who turned this story into comedy, realised this didnt just have to be a story about war. It could be a topical satire on Britains public school elites as well. There was also room to explore grief, longing and feminism. One of the complaints we hear most often is that so many of our groups listed on the website are full and members cant join the one theyre interested in. We are acutely aware of this and, as well as sup- porting existing groups, we have been working hard to encourage new groups to start, to meet the ever- increasing demand. We recently carried out a brief analysis of our data and, to our surprise, found that of our 71 existing groups, 19 are new groups started in the last twelve months. This publication has limited space, so we wont list them here, but in next months magazine we will set you a puzzle to work them out for yourselves! Between January 2025 and January 2026, our membership rose by about 100. However, the new groups between them are now accommo- dating 373 members, so overall we have increased the opportunity for both new and existing members. Sometimes of course, a new group proves instantly popular and quickly becomes full, but in some instances this has led to a waiting list from which a second group has started, so it is always worth registering your interest. It would be unrealistic to expect we will ever have a situation where there are spaces available in every group, but we hope you will see that we are far from complacent, and our thanks go to those members who have come forward recently, and to those who have been working tirelessly for several years, to provide the wide variety of activities on offer in St Ives u3a. If you have an idea for a new group, and are willing to help us set it up, please let us know. gst@u3aivo.uk

Around St Ives 8 Screen St Ives, Corn Exchange Thursday 5th February 7.30pm NHS Hearing Aid Maintenance Wednesday 11th Feb @ 10:00 am - 12:00 pm, St Ives Library Cambridge Deaf Association Our free hearing aid maintenance sessions are designed to help you keep your NHS hearing aids working at their best. At our friendly community drop-ins, trained volunteers can retube, replace and provide batteries, clean and check hearing aids, and offer advice on how to get the most from them. There is no need to book, simply come along. Each session is relaxed and welcoming, giving you the chance to have your hearing aid looked after while meeting others in your community.
